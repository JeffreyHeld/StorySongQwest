# Qwest Product Vision

> **Documentation class: Canonical.** This document records the governing
> product principles established by the StoryQwest / SongQwest Product Vision
> Interview completed on 2026-07-13. It is the product authority for what the
> Qwest medium should feel like and why it exists. `docs/product-language.md`
> remains canonical for terminology, and `docs/architecture-map.md` remains
> canonical for repository ownership and feature routing.

## Purpose

StoryQwest and SongQwest exist because novels, albums, films, audiobooks, games,
and websites each require a creator to surrender part of an imagined experience.
The Qwest medium allows story, music, interaction, memory, discovery, atmosphere,
and agency to coexist while preserving the audience's imagination.

The objective is not to add more interactivity to existing media. It is to create
a medium in which technology recedes and a person forms a credible relationship
with a place they can inhabit.

```text
The interface exists, but it is dressed as part of the world.
```

## The Design Constitution

### Presence Before Software

A person should think about the world, not menus, features, plugins, timelines,
inventories, achievements, or software controls. Interface is permitted and
necessary, but its expression must belong to the fiction. Atmosphere supports
presence without replacing imagination or demanding attention for itself.

### Attention, Not Interaction

The medium does not reward interaction for its own sake. It honors attention.
The ideal response to curiosity feels like a conversation with the world:

```text
You noticed that too.
```

A discovery matters when it deepens meaning, changes understanding, or connects
to something remembered later. It does not matter merely because it was hidden
or added to a collection.

### Trust Before Instruction

A Qwest does not teach controls through a conventional tutorial. A person's
first successful interaction teaches a more important promise:

```text
When something in this world feels significant, it is worth paying attention.
```

The medium teaches trust through story-native cues and meaningful responses.
Different people may learn that promise through different moments.

### A Shared Grammar, A World-Specific Dialect

The platform owns a stable interaction grammar. People should carry forward
their understanding of deliberate attention, reveals, discoveries, choices,
journals, maps, cast context, and other Qwest behaviors.

Each world owns the dialect. The same underlying action may appear as blue flame
uncovering a rune, graphite revealing an indentation, distorted text bleeding
through a page, faded historical ink becoming legible, or another expression
native to the fiction. Qwest branding must not be stamped visibly across every
interaction.

Creator expression is bounded by platform promises: interactions remain
discoverable, similar actions do not produce contradictory results, progress is
predictable, and keyboard and assistive alternatives preserve the same meaning.

### The World Is A Place, Not A Service

A credible world has history, beauty, tragedy, silence, and mystery that do not
exist solely for the audience. Some carvings are only carvings. Some doors remain
closed. Some conversations never occur. Some mysteries are never solved.

The world must sometimes remain silent even when someone pays attention. If
every unusual detail responds, curiosity becomes a search mechanic, expectation
becomes optimization, and the world becomes an interface.

Restraint gives acknowledgement weight. The world does not exist to entertain a
person every second; it exists to be inhabited.

### Depth Before Prizes

The best response does not necessarily give someone more. It gives them deeper.
A journal changes how a person understands a character. A letter changes the
meaning of a later sacrifice. A symbol or lyric remembered hours later creates
the quiet realization:

```text
Wait. I remember this.
```

Points, reward fanfare, collectible anxiety, visible completion pressure, and
engagement loops redirect motivation from curiosity to optimization. They are
incompatible with the medium when they become the reason to explore.

Achievements, if retained at all, are governed by the same rule. They may
function only as quiet authored recognition or meaningful remembrance that
deepens a person's relationship to the world. They must not become points,
required completion goals, persistent progress pressure, external reward loops,
or a checklist that competes with the story. An achievement that cannot meet
this standard does not belong in the active product.

### Ownership Of A Journey, Not Control Of A World

Agency defines a person's relationship to the world. It does not grant power to
rewrite the universe around them.

Choices may change trust, fear, companionship, conversations, places visited,
songs heard, discoveries made, understanding, misunderstanding, and what the
protagonist carries forward. History, weather, grief, time, other people's
desires, and consequential events may remain beyond control.

The desired experience is not:

```text
I changed the world.
```

It is:

```text
The world changed me.
```

Agency may also be implicit. Attention, silence, lingering, refusing to inspect
something, or moving on can shape a journey without announcing that a branch or
decision occurred.

### The Reader Accompanies The Protagonist

The reader neither replaces nor merely observes the protagonist. The creator
authors a real person with a past, relationships, strengths, flaws, obligations,
desires, and limits. The reader performs that person within an authentic range,
as different actors can truthfully interpret the same character.

Every offered choice must be something the protagonist could genuinely do and
something the world could honestly allow. Truthfulness does not require
predictability; a character may surprise themselves by becoming someone they
could honestly become. A few emotionally truthful possibilities are more
valuable than many mechanically different or contradictory options.

Some moments should offer no choice. Loss, change, awakening, sacrifice, and
history can establish the conditions under which agency gains meaning.

### StoryQwest Preserves Imagination

StoryQwest remains reading-first. Prose and the audience's imagination carry the
world. Music, ambience, environmental change, media, reveals, discoveries,
journals, maps, cast context, and choices deepen that world without fixing every
face, creature, and place into one visual interpretation or turning the story
into a game interface.

### SongQwest Makes Music Narrative

SongQwest remains listening-first. Music is not background and the product is
not a web page with an embedded player. Songs, lyrics, environmental change,
reveals, discoveries, choices, maps, cast context, and remembered state form one
narrative performance. Music may carry meaning across the journey and the path
may determine which completed song comes next.

SongQwest begins with release-ready music created outside the platform. It is
not a recording, waveform-editing, stem-editing, mixing, mastering, MIDI, or
live-production environment.

### Accessibility Preserves Meaning

Accessibility is part of the medium's grammar, not a separate mode. Keyboard,
screen-reader, reduced-motion, contrast, transcript, caption, and other
alternatives must preserve narrative meaning and trustworthy behavior even when
the world's visual or sonic dialect changes.

## Emotional Standard

The desired emotional response is quiet wonder rather than victory:

- “I'm glad I was paying attention.”
- “This world is deeper than I realized.”
- “That meant something all along.”
- “I was truly present here.”

The strongest experience is one that could only have emerged through presence.
The audience should remember the world and their relationship to it, not the
software that delivered it.

## Product Decision Test

Every proposed feature, milestone, surface, and platform service must answer:

1. Does it strengthen presence, attention, credible agency, emotional
   continuity, creator expression, accessibility, or the relationship between
   person and place?
2. Can it remain subordinate to the world, or will it make the audience think
   about operating software?
3. Does it deepen curiosity, or convert curiosity into optimization?
4. Does it preserve the integrity of the protagonist and the independence of
   the world?
5. Is it necessary to create, validate, publish, deliver, resume, or safely
   experience the medium?

Work that cannot pass this test is deferred even when it is technically useful,
commercially conventional, or already present in an older roadmap.

## Explicit Non-Goals

- Conventional tutorials that interrupt the fiction to explain controls.
- Persistent software chrome or branded interaction styling across every world.
- Interaction for interaction's sake.
- Guaranteed responses to every attentive act.
- Points, reward fanfare, visible completion pressure, or collectible anxiety as
  the motivation to explore.
- Arbitrary player freedom that contradicts the protagonist or the world.
- Worlds that continually rearrange themselves to gratify the audience.
- Visual spectacle that replaces literary imagination.
- Marketplace, community, social, analytics, or infrastructure expansion that
  is not required to realize the medium.
- Music-production scope inside SongQwest.
